Frequency Spectrum

Delta Waves

Frequency: 0.5-4 Hz

Amplitude: High (100-200 ¬µV)

State: Deep Sleep, Unconsciousness

Characteristics:

Delta waves are the slowest brainwaves and are associated with deep, dreamless sleep. They have the highest amplitude and are crucial for healing and regeneration.

Clinical Significance:

  • Present during stages 3 and 4 of NREM sleep
  • Associated with growth hormone release
  • Important for memory consolidation
  • Abnormal delta activity in awake states may indicate brain injury

How to Read EEG Data

1. Understanding Frequency

EEG measures electrical activity in the brain. The frequency (Hz) tells us how many times per second the brain waves oscillate. Different frequencies correspond to different mental states.

2. Amplitude Analysis

Amplitude (measured in microvolts - ¬µV) indicates the strength of the brain activity. Higher amplitudes often correlate with synchronized neural firing.

3. Electrode Placement

The 10-20 system standardizes electrode placement. Each position corresponds to specific brain regions, helping localize brain activity.

4. Pattern Recognition

Learn to identify characteristic patterns: sleep spindles, K-complexes, epileptiform discharges, and normal rhythmic activity.
